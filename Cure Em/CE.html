Cure'Em

This was a prototype game for the Microsoft swedish Game Camp 2023 about curing zombies with friends. The idea came from the fact that all group members really like couch co-op games where chaos thrives. The players will collaborate to pick up and stop funnel zombies into the curing machine in order to get more workers.

Contributions

For this project I did most of the programming as well as taking care of the GUI. The main goal while programming was modularity and scalability. We knew that we would want a ton of possibilities when it came to interactables and functionality so making this process easy was very important.

The Players

Movement is momentum-based and fairly quick so that players can control the zombies in the smoothest way possible. The players can interact with gadgets around the map by walking inside their "interaction circles" and pressing a key. They can also pick up zombies, doing so while standing in front of them will cause a quick-time event to pop up. Succeeding will pick up the zombie, failing will stun the player.

The Zombies

These move very simply by following the player and breaking down potential obstacles in their way. When they get picked up they enter a ragdoll state and can be flung around (or into others), getting back up once released. By forcing the zombies into the "Cure machine", they will transform into scientists and be thrown out.
